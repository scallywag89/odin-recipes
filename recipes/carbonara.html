Spaghetti Carbonara

A delicious Roman classic

Carbonara is an Italian pasta dish from Rome made with eggs, hard cheese, cured pork, and black pepper. The dish arrived at its modern form, with its current name, in the middle of the 20th century. The cheese is usually Pecorino Romano, Parmigiano-Reggiano, or a combination of the two.

Ingredients

For the Sauce

  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 150g flat pancetta
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 100g parmesan cheese
  • cracked black pepper

To Serve

  • Spaghetti
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Separate egg yolks and beat together with the parmesan cheese and cracked black pepper. Set aside in a bowl
  2. Cook the spaghetti as per the packet instructions.
  3. While the spaghetti is cooking, slice the pancetta into lardons. Gently fry with the garlic and a little olive oil until the fat has rendered.
  4. Drain the spaghetti, reserving 1/2 cup of cooking water and return to the pan with the cooked pancetta. Remove the garlic and stir the spaghetti to coat in the flavours.
  5. Remove the pan from the heat and add the egg and cheese mixture to the pan. Stir the sauce until it is a creamy consistency, loosed with some reserved pasta water if too thick.
  6. Serve immediately, bon appetito!
